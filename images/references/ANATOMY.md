# Squibbon Anatomy Reference

## Physical Characteristics

### Body Structure
- Small, vaguely oval-shaped torso
- No skeleton (boneless body allows extreme flexibility)
- Eight long, flexible legs (like a squid)
- Two pairs of smaller tentacles with developed suckers

### Eyes
- Large eyes set on muscular, flexible stalks
- Eyes stay level with body's center of gravity during movement
- Sharp vision enables high-speed canopy navigation

### Tentacles
- Suction cups form fingerlike/pincerlike protuberances
- Highly flexible, capable of manipulating small objects
- Can use simple tools and weapons

### Size
- Small enough to be arboreal
- Able to be held/carried (based on cartoon depiction)

## Movement

### Somersaulting Brachiation
- Unique locomotion style - loops end-over-end
- Continuous somersaulting action through trees
- More effective than gibbon-style arm-over-arm swinging
- Eyes stay level during movement to track next branch

## Behavior

### Social Structure
- Live in family communes
- Build simple nest-like structures in upper canopy
- Complex family bonds and social structures
- Work together as a community

### Intelligence
- Most intelligent animals of 200 million AD
- Capable of using tools
- Young learn by playing
- Use branches as clubs, fruits as projectiles
- May develop civilization in the far future

### Diet
- Omnivorous
- Plants, fruits, lichen pods
- Small animals (caught with tentacles)

## Color/Appearance (from various depictions)
- Documentary: Reddish-brown/orange coloration
- Cartoon (Squibby): Purple/violet with lighter underside
- Large expressive eyes (cartoon) or stalked eyes (documentary)
